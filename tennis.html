Rules-Singles

In singles it is a game of 1 versus 1 where the opponents are positioned diagonally on the courts. Who serves is usually determined by the spin of a racket.

  • For singles, the game is played only in the singles area, balls that bounce outside the court or into the doubles area are out.
  • There are two chances two serve, if both serves are out of bounds then the opponenet gets the point.
  • Serves always start on the right side and must enter the service box diagonal from the server.
  • After each point

Rules-Doubles

In doubles it is a game of 2 versus 2 where the opponents are positioned in the same position as singles while the two additional people are positioned up at the net in the service box not currently receiving the serve.

  • For doubles, the game is played in the whole court
  • The server moves from the right to left side of the service line and the person at the net moves from left to right at the net after each point.
  • The returners move back and forth from their position returning to the net.
  • All other rules are the same as singles

Points

The points start at love(0) all,then goes to 15, 30,40, and game(won that game).

The hierarchy starts at points, then up to game, then set, then match(win!).

In Women's tennis it goes best of three sets win 6 games by two.

If both players are at 40 points then it is called deuce. After that, two points have to be won in a row. AD out(advantage out) means the opponent has 1 point in a row. AD in(advantage in) means the server has 1 point in a row.
